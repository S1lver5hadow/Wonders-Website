Explore an Endless Wall

The Great Wall Of China describes a wall that stretches across the northern borders of ancient Chinese states for protection. The walls themselves were built across different dynasties who would maintain and add to the wall as needed. Its uses were not only limited to defence, maintaining and regulating trade was a major use of the walls in the past. Today much of the wall is in disrepair with only roughly 20% suitable for tourism. Despite this, it still remains one of the most popular tourist attractions in China and is a wonder of the world.

Found in northern China
Walls were built from as early as the 7th century BC
The first emperor of China (QSH) connected these walls together
Spans over 20,000km (12,000mi) in total
The average height of each wall is 6 to 7m
The wall can be seen from space
Despite being a massive wall, it wasn't very successful in defence
It is the Longest Cemetery in the world
Building the wall was a punishment and 400 thousand out of the 1 million workers died
