Explore a Religious Statue

A symbol of Christianity worldwide can be found ontop of Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Christ the Redeemer was built between 1922 and 1931 to help cure the "godlessness" in society at the time. The donations came from Brazilian Catholics and the French sculptor Paul Landowski made the work. Recently the statue has been damaged due to stray lightning strikes and erosion however restoration work is ongoing.

Found at the peak of Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
Built between 1922 and 1931
Built using reinforced concrete and an outershell of 6 million soapstone tiles
It's believed that the workers put hidden messages on the inside of some of these tiles
The original design intended Christ to hold a globe in one hand and a cross in the other
It was not orginally built in Brazil
Paul made it in France in clay pieces which was then shipped to Brazil to be remade with concrete
Cost $250,000 to build
It is the tallest art deco statue in the world
Housing prices in Rio increase if the property has even a small view of the statue
