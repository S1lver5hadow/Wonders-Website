Explore a Mythical Mayan City

Chichén Itzá is the name of a Mayan city found in Mexico. It was one of the largest Mayan cities and was refered to as a mythical city (Tollan) in later Mesoamerican literature. This city did not have a single time period in which it existed and instead was built upon since 550 AD up until 1550AD. In the past settlers were drawn there due to the easy access to water thanks to the caves and sinkholes. Now the site is famous among archaeologists and tourists for its history and artifacts.

Built between 550AD and 1550AD
Found in Yucatán, Mexico
The name "Chichén Itzá" means "the mouth at the well of Itza"
It is believed that Itza means "water magicians"
The pyramid found in the city is called El Castillo (the Temple of Kukulkan)
It actually sits ontop of a much older temple
During the Spring and Autumn Equinox sunrays create a shadow across the Kukulkan pyramid giving the appearance of a serpent slithering down the staircase
Kukulkan is the name of a Mesoamerican serpent diety
Although the Kukulkan pyramid is the most famous, there are actually many other pyramids as well
Many of the sites in Chichén Itzá produce different sounds depending on where you clap
A clap in front of the Kukulkan pyramid produces an echo resembling a serpent's chirp
