Explore a Large Amphitheatre

The Romans are well-known by all and their amphitheatres are just as famous, among these The Colosseum stands out as the most popular one. In the centre of the great city of Rome lies a building made out of limestone, tuff and concrete that was built almost 2000 years ago. In the past the building held gladiatoral shows as well as other events. Now The Colosseum is a major tourist attraction, a museum dedicated to the God Eros and finally a site for Roman Catholic ceremonies.

Built in 70 AD
Found in Rome,Italy
The building has 80 entrances and can seat 50,000 spectators
Major events were often free and paid for by the emperor
During certain games, 10,000 animals were killed in a single day
The first games were held in 80AD
These games lasted 100 days straight and games were held for centuries to come
Below the arena are numerous rooms and passages
These were used to hold animals and gladiators, it was opened to the public in 2010
